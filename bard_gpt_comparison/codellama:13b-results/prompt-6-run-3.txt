Who invented video games?

The invention of video games is a complex and ongoing process that has involved the contributions of many individuals and organizations over the years. While it is difficult to identify a single inventor or date for the creation of video games, some notable milestones in the history of video gaming include:

* 1947: The first electronic game, called "Tennis for Two," was developed by physicist William Higinbotham at the Brookhaven National Laboratory. This game used a television set as a display and required players to control a racket using a joystick.
* 1958: The first arcade game, called "Pong," was released by Atari in the United States. Pong was a simple game that involved controlling a paddle on a screen to hit a ball back and forth.
* 1962: The first home video game console, called the Magnavox Odyssey, was released in the United States. This console allowed players to play a variety of games, including Pong and other simple games.
* 1972: The first commercial arcade video game, called "Space Invaders," was released by Taito in Japan. Space Invaders became an instant hit and helped popularize the concept of shooting games.
* 1976: The first home video game console, called the Atari 2600, was released in the United States. This console became one of the most popular game systems of all time and introduced many popular games, including Pac-Man and Donkey Kong.
* 1985: The first graphical video game, called "Super Mario Bros.," was released by Nintendo for the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES). This game featured a 2D graphical style and became one of the most popular games of all time.

Overall, the history of video games is a complex and ongoing process that has involved the contributions of many individuals and organizations over the years.
